SUB-MERGED CONFLICTS

SUB-MERGED CONFLICTS is a co-op deep sea adventure game, where you play as two scuba divers
who must fend off enemies and find the treasure!
Made by Christian Foreman, Manish Venumuddula, Adam Scovera, and Andrew Scheffer

  • Play Now

Development

SUB-MERGED CONFLICTS was made in 7 weeks as a major design project at the University of Michigan. We created this game with C# and Unity and used Aseprite for the art and jsfxr to generate a majority of the sound effects.

Gameplay

This game is a hectic local co-op game where the two players must coordinate to survive. They can use turrets to kill enemies, the shield to defend themselves, the ebike to charge the sub, the pump to get rid of water, the robot arm to collect materials, and more. Explore the deep sea to find hidden treasures and perhaps even survive to tell the tale.

My Contributions

As for my contributions to this game, I mainly focused on implementing: water pump, radar, crab boss, boids, pixelart, transitions, effects.

SnowKid

SnowKid is a top-down shooter roguelike where you play as a snowman
who must fend off the waves of fire enemies. How long can you survive?
Made by Christian Foreman

  • Play Now

Development

SnowKid was made in less than two weeks with Unity and C#. I used Aseprite to create the pixelart assets and animations and sfxr.me to generate sound effects.

Gameplay

In SnowKid, you play as a snowman who must fight several different types of fire enemies to survive. Every few waves, buy upgrades from a randomized shop to increase your chances of survival

Mechanics

The projectiles function in a very unique way in SnowKid. SnowKid's snowball bullets infinitely bounce off walls and only break on impact with other entities. When snowballs and fireballs collide, water droplets spawn which can be used as currency in the shop. These water droplets and ammo pickups also function as weapons as when enemies walk into them, they take damage. The unique interactions with projectiles make for a very chaotic and fun top-down shooting experience!

The Legend of Duck (Zelda Unity Recreation)

Video game design project by Andrew Scheffer and Christian Foreman that
implements the Legend of Zelda's first dungeon in addition to a custom level

  • Play Now

Christian Foreman's Contributions

I worked on player/enemy movement, knockback logic, collectables, the UI, and sound. In the custom level, I created the art, designed the levels, and worked on the cutscenes.

Technologies Used

This project uses the Unity game engine to recreate the first dungeon in Zelda. Our team used C# and Unity's component-style architecture throughout the project

Custom Level

For our custom level, our team implimented a duck controller. Every movement the player makes is mirrored by the duck which makes for very interesting puzzles and dangerous situations in our custom level!
